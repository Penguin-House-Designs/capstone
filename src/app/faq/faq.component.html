As a buyer, it is important for you to have an inspection done on the home you are considering. Think of this like a “test drive” on a car you are buying. Let us test drive your home, to make sure we are detecting minor issues before they become major.

 What is radon?

Radon is a cancer-causing radioactive gas that is formed from the natural breakdown of uranium. You cannot see, smell or taste radon, but it may be a problem in your home. The Surgeon General has warned that radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States today. If you smoke and your home has high radon levels, you’re at high risk for developing lung cancer. Some scientific studies of radon exposure indicate that children may be more sensitive to radon.

 Should I get a Radon test?

Testing is the only way to know your home’s radon levels. There are no immediate symptoms that will alert you to the presence of radon. It typically takes years of exposure before any problems surface. The US EPA, Surgeon General, American Lung Association, American Medical Association, and National Safety Council recommend testing your home for radon because testing is the only way to know your home’s radon levels.
